## The old barrier is gone

Before fractional shares, expensive stocks were out of reach for small investors. NVDA at $900 meant you needed $900 minimum just to own a single share. Now you can invest $10, $50, or $100 in any stock and own exactly that fraction.

## How to do it in this app

On the trade page, look at the **Quantity** section. You'll see two buttons on the right: **Shares** and **Dollars**.

Switch to **Dollars**. A `$` sign appears in the input. Now type in how much you want to invest — not how many shares.

**Example:** You type `$100` and hit Buy on NVDA at $900 per share.
- You'll receive **0.111 shares**
- Your position is worth $100
- If NVDA rises 10%, your position becomes $110 — same return as someone who owns 100 shares

## Why this matters

Dollar-based investing lets you:

- **Diversify with any amount** — put $50 into 5 different stocks instead of being forced into one whole share
- **Invest fixed amounts regularly** — $100 every week, regardless of price fluctuations
- **Not wait until you have "enough"** — start immediately with what you have

## The math is the same

Whether you own 1 share or 0.001 shares, your percentage gain and loss is identical. You own a proportional slice of the company.
