## What closing means

When you close a position, you sell the shares you own. Your unrealized P&L becomes realized — it's either in your cash balance (if you profited) or gone (if you lost). There's no going back.

You need to have at least one open position to complete this lesson. If your portfolio is empty, place a buy order first.

## Full close vs partial close

On the position detail page you'll see two options:

**Close All** — sells every share you own in that stock. Position is gone, cash returned.

**Partial Close** — sells only a portion. A shares input appears — you type how many shares to sell. The rest of your position stays open.

## When to use each

**Close All** when:
- You've hit your profit target
- You've decided the thesis is wrong and want out completely
- You need the cash for another trade

**Partial Close** when:
- You want to lock in some profit while letting the rest run
- The position has grown too large relative to your portfolio and you want to size it down
- You're not fully committed either way

## The emotional part

Closing positions is the hardest part of trading. Selling a winner feels premature ("what if it keeps going?"). Selling a loser feels like admitting a mistake. 

There's no perfect answer. But making the decision and executing is a skill on its own — and one this lesson will give you practice with.
